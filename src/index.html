Ten Things You Should Know About Medicare
  • Apply your Medicare Card (Part A & Part B), when turn 65 or disability at Social Security Administration Office.
  • There are two ways to get Medicare: Original Medicare, provided by federal government or by choosing a Medicare Advantage Plan (Part C). They are offered by private insurance companies.
  • With Original Medicare, you will pay a share of the cost.
  • Medicare Supplement insurance helps to cover the medical costs gap.
  • Prescription drug coverage helps limit drug costs.
  • Know the different choices of Plans available in your area.
  • Enroll at the right time. The Initial Enrollment Period (IEP).
  • Review your choices yearly during Open Enrollment Period (OEP), October 15 to December 7.
  • Special Election Period (SEP).
  • Review your present coverage - working beyond 65.
